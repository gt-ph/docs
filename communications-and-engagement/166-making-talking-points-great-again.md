## Full Write-Up

# Problem Statement
"How might we transform the current manual, fragmented and time-consuming user journey for drafting Senior Leaderships' talking points into a seamless AI-powered experience that simplifies the process while pulling together information across multiple platforms?"
## Users
Primarily CMG, P&E, CE's Special Assistants. However, this tool can also be used by any other teams involved in preparing senior leaders’ talking points. Anyone who speaks publicly as a GovTech representative.
## Use Case
Users are preparing talking points for GovTech senior leaders' public speaking engagements, typically requiring examples to support organisation-level narratives. They could also be preparing presentation materials for themselves.
Each team supporting senior management works in a silo with no visibility or straightforward transfer of potentially useful information that has already been cleared to use. The teams don’t know what they don’t know, as they lack the big picture view of the organisation that senior leaders possess by
default. Also lost is any guidance given by senior management to one team on the structure or content of the talking points. This leads to repeated requests from one team to another for the same information and/or drafts submitted to senior management omitting relevant information that already exists.
Other GovTechies who speak publicly are often expected to share about GovTech as an organisation, not just their individual job scopes. At times like this, being able to align with information and organisational narratives
approved for public consumption is ideal.
## Pain Point
Existing information cannot be found or repurposed easily by teams that function in silo:
·      Teams that prepare CE's talking points can only share knowledge manually by sending each other the materials prepared or providing input when asked. This is a menial task that increases lead time (time taken to see the request, time taken to escalate request to superiors, time taken to craft response).
·       Teams also do not share knowledge by default because we are siloed and have different business needs/objectives. Some information is good to share but some must be kept within the team for operational reasons. Deciding what is ok to share (e.g. extracting non-sensitive slides from CE's deck) is an additional
step in an already laborious process, and there is no incentive for teams to do so except as a favour.
## Consequence
●     Users waste time (up to 2 weeks) manually searching for information that may already exist,
ready-to-use, in another part of the organisation.
●     Talking points and presentation materials that are not as sharp as they could be, diminishing the strategic
impact of speaking engagements undertaken by GovTechies especially senior management.
# User Research and Market Analysis
## User Research
We conducted user surveys with ~15 GovTechies who are/were involved in senior management communications or have had experience drafting talking points to find out their user journey.
Most respondents highlighted the manual process of going through multiple touchpoints (‘1000 pings’) to source for necessary information to draft talking points
- 57% of respondents spent 3-5 working days on average developing each talking point draft, with 7% spending more than 2 weeks
- ~85% of respondents identified scattered information as their main pain point
- ~70% also said uncertainty about data accuracy was a problem
- 42% highlighted issues with no single source of truth (a repository) and a lack of strategic direction/context

## De-risking
Our product hinges on the assumption that it takes a long time to craft talking points because GovTechies cannot easily find and contextualise information. A tool that only finds information is a search engine; a tool that crafts talking points must understand intent.
Our user research established that users want a tool that serves not just as a crawler but can also select the right information specific to their needs.

# Solutioning
## Problem-Solution Fit
An AI assistant locates and contextualises the information with high accuracy for talking point drafts.
## Functionality
1)   Backend information pipeline that can trawl through  various
platforms where the bulk of information resides e.g. Sharepoint, Slack, GWS
2)   Frontend that can identify the speaking format or context (e.g. external keynote, closed-door meeting with vendor), make sense of the topic, and provide relevant information for the audience

# Impact & Next Steps
## Success Metrics
Within GovTech:
●   For a start, 3 or more identified function teams benefit from easily discoverable information that is contextualised E.g. CMG can repurpose information used in internal decks prepared by SAs, or junior GovTechies can repurpose information shared by CE for their own speaking engagements
Stretch Goals:
●   The tool is versatile enough to be repurposed for officers across the entire public service
●   The tool is versatile enough to draft other public-facing materials beyond speeches/presentations, such as media releases or factsheets
## Expected Impact
●   Reduce the time taken for each user to craft their draft talking points for a speech from
the current average of 3 – 5 working days to 1 working day (not including time for clearance)
●   > 70% info accuracy in the AI generated drafts before fact checking
