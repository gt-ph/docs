## Full Write-Up

# Problem Statement
## Users
Talent Acquisition Partners, all incoming candidates
## Use Case
Talent Acquisition (TA) partners are overwhelmed by high-volume applications but often find traditional AI screening tools "rigid" or "too technical." When hiring requirements shift, as they often do in agile environments, non-technical recruiters cannot easily re-calibrate the AI, leading to mismatched candidates or a total abandonment of the tool in favor of manual (and slow) screening.

An intuitive, No-Code AI Recruiter that empowers TA partners to operationalize resume screening. It uses a natural language interface to set, weigh, and adjust role-specific requirements on the fly, transforming raw resumes into a ranked shortlist, while allowing TA to eventually decide on if the resumes "shortlisted" or are "rejected."
## Pain Point
The "Rigid Script" Problem: Existing screening bots in the market require complex backend adjustments and are time consuming. As such, past AI bots or past AI screening vendors have never succeeded in adhering to our continuously developing requirements. 

Technical Barrier: TA partners lack the coding skills to "fine-tune" LLM prompts or code manually.
## Consequence
High Time-to-Hire where Manual screening can take 15 hours on average a week

Candidate Ghosting: Top talent is lost to competitors because the initial screening phase is too slow.

Opportunity cost: Time spent on manual screening of resumes can actually be better spent on more value-added tasks.

Data: Currently, manual screening would only lead to shortlisting candidates for interview, but the remaining resumes will be left behind and unrecorded. This data is actually indicative of the demographics of talent that govtech attracts and can give us insights into how we can better position ourselves and how we can better attract the talent we are actively seeking for.
# User Research and Market Analysis
## User Research
Job analysis to observe how TA partners manually scan resumes for shortlisting and dug into past failed AI implementations to see exactly where the logic diverged from recruiter intuition.
- Recruiters often look for transferable skills or markers that traditional keyword-based ATS bots miss.
- Recruiters feel locked in once a job description is posted. They need to be able to tell the AI what the Must-haves and good-to-haves as and when they require it.
## De-risking
- Hallucination risk: LLMs can sometimes invent candidate experience. we mitigate this by ensuring CVision provides evidence-based summaries.
## Stakeholder Buy-in
Partnered with TA Partners to make sure CVision integrates seamlessly into their workflows.
# Solutioning
## Problem-Solution Fit
- CVision replaces the "Rigid Script" with Dynamic prompting. Instead of coding, TA partners can use an interface to educate the bot on what the Must-haves and good-to-haves according to the roles are, effectively lowering the technical barrier.
## Functionality
- Natural language prompting
- Snapshot and evidence-based summary
- A simple interface for TAs without them needing to navigate in and out of different candidates to review the resumes.
## User Experience
Made the candidates resumes navigation seamless. This would allow TAs to move through the AI analysis of the resumes with ease.
## User Validation
- TA Partners, including those not confident in AI analysis, have more control and act as the final decision maker for the outcome of the candidate's resume.
- Early Testers are also asking for the same AI summary to be shared with Hiring Managers as well to let them see why the TAs are making such decisions in shortlisting candidates.
# Impact & Next Steps
## Success Metrics
- Hours saved on resume screening
- Time to hire reduced
## Expected Impact
Short term: Reduction of manual screening time.
Medium term: Enrichment of the talent data, where 100% of applicants data is captured to identify hiring trends and demographic gaps.
Long term: Predictive hiring, using CVision data to predict which candidate profiles are most likely to stay at the organisation for >2 years.
