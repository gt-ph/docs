## Full Write-Up

PITCH DECK: https://go.gov.sg/build2026-reclaim
1. The Basics
● Product Name: Reclaim
● Product One-liner (&lt; 150 words): 150 overseas staff spend 30 mins form-filling per expense claim, diverting time from high-value work to admin for work-related cost reimbursements.
2.Problem Statement
● Users: 150 overseas staff in EnterpriseSG
● Use Case: Staff submit expense claims to be reimbursed for their work-related costs they have personally paid for (e.g. work trips/entertainment expenses), on an average of twice a month. They currently do this over a claim submission portal.
● Pain Point: Each staff spends ~30 minutes per expense claim submission filling in the claim form, attaching supporting documents and cross-checking with the policy to ensure that the correct inputs are provided.
● Consequence: This results in up to 150 man-hours (30min x 150 staff x average 2 times) per month spent on administrative activities, diverting away valuable time from higher-value work.

3. User Research and Market Analysis
● User Research: User research was conducted to validate the need for the proposed solution and to understand key pain points faced by overseas staff. This comprised user surveys and interviews involving 16 overseas staff from different unique cities and followed up with focus group discussions.
Key insights from survey respondents include:
- 50% of respondents indicated that they spend approximately 30 minutes per claim on manual form filling, despite using a claims system with OCR capabilities.
- 75% of respondents reported that claims submission is tedious due to the need to repeatedly crosscheck policies, alongside an excessive number of mandatory fields required in the current claims system.
● De-risking:
- Users want this feature: The chore to cross-check policies, key in the inputs into the existing claim system, and validating what the OCR had picked up is time consuming and painful enough. Such that overseas staffs are willing to explore alternative solutions with better user experience. User interview to validate pain-point and whether they actually want this.
- Users know how to use the feature: Usability testing via lab testing to observe user behaviour through the use of the prototype.
- Solutions available in market: Yes, there are SaaS/COTS products which are available for use. Currently, EnterpriseSG is utilising a SaaS claim system. However, it is not fully customisable which led to the current pain-points.
● Stakeholder Buy-in: EnterpriseSG Finance Team – Yes, they had claimed Business Owner role for this problem statement.

4. Solutioning
● Problem-Solution Fit: A fully customisable AI & OCR enabled claim system which automatically reads receipts/attachments to complete the necessary fields in the claim form and cross-checks the inputs against the uploaded claim policies, thereby reducing users time spent on claim submission.
● Functionality:
1. User logs into system
1. User uploads receipt into system, system auto-populates claim form fields
1. User checks claim form fields for accuracy
1. User fills in additional information that is not available from AI/OCR scans
1. User submits the claim
● User Experience: To make the experience frictionless, users will only need to upload receipts/documents and fill in limited fields to complete their claim process. This is without the need to cross-check policies.
● User Validation: Users were invited to test the prototype. Feedback obtained:
- Drag and drop for attachments requested
- Amendments to claim form submission should enforce a re-run of policy checks
- Improve meal-type logic via time clustering; add dropdown for manual override
- AI auto-populated fields should be distinctly labelled for user awareness

5. Impact & Next Steps
● Success Metrics:
- Time spent per claim submission – A low number indicates that we are achieving meaningful time savings for overseas staff (i.e. Reclaim Time!)
- CSAT Score – A high number indicates that overseas staff are happy with the new feature
● Expected Impact: We plan to onboard 30 overseas staff for a pilot, with the goal of reducing time spent on claim submission from 30 minutes to 8 minutes per submission.
