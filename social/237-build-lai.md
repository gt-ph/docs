## Summary

Turn the fitness corner downstairs into your training ground. GetStrong! shows you what to do, with a personalised plan for your goals and lifestyle.

## Full Write-Up

# Problem Statement
## Users
Singapore residents who want to start strength training but have not. 21% of survey respondents (n=255 of 1203) self-identify as "thinking of starting but haven't yet". This is our primary persona.
## Use Case
MOPs want to exercise conveniently near their homes. However, they don't know which corner is right for them, what equipment is there, or how to make use of it to meet their fitness goals.
## Pain Point
Three barriers from our survey:
- 42% don't know what exercises to do at a corner.
- 35% feel corners "don't suit my goals."
- 18% don't think of the corner as an option in the moment. The infrastructure is there, but residents don't have a path to use it.
## Consequence
If left unsolved, public fitness corners will remain underutilized community assets (HDB fitness corner usage fell from 41% in 2022 to 19% in 2025 (NSPS 2025)). More importantly, residents miss out on a free, convenient way to stay active and maintain their mobility. We also miss the opportunity to connect offline neighborhood activities with digital health ecosystems like Healthy365 to encourage sustained habits.
# User Research and Market Analysis
## User Research
We surveyed 1,203 Singapore residents in May 2026, with structured questions and free-text responses on motivations, barriers, and perceptions of fitness corners. We also drew on the NSPS 2025 for longitudinal validation. Two key findings shaped the design:
- Barriers to exercising more are intrinsic, not infrastructural. Top reasons are:
  - lack of motivation (25.5%),
  - no time (22.6%),
  - and not having a routine that fits (12.9%).
- For our target persona (people who want to start, but haven't), they need more clarity on how to use the equipment (22%) and which fitness corners are the most convenient that fits their goals (20%).
We chose this segment because it's a significant group (21% of survey respondents), and they're motivated enough to be reachable, and just stuck on barriers that a digital intervention can directly address.
To move the needle, residents just need to know how fitness corners actually can meet their goals.
To move the needle, fitness corners need to feel relevant and goal 51% of residents are neutral towards fitness corners — they are aware of them and have dismissed them as "not for me right now." This is a conversion problem, not an awareness problem.
## De-risking
Our product hinges on three main risks:
- Existing fitness apps (Healthy 365, ActiveSG) already address general fitness motivation. 
GetStrong! Singapore is not a generic fitness app — it is a physical-asset activation layer tied to specific neighbourhood corners. No existing product matches users to corners with corner-specific routines.
- Behavioural change is hard to evidence in a hackathon window. 
We anchored the design in two established frameworks — the COM-B model and the Habit Loop — so the theory of change is defensible even before quantitative outcomes are measurable.
- Corner equipment data is fragmented across agencies and Town Councils. 
For the prototype, we curated 4 real corners in the North with verified equipment data. In production, we would propose partnering with HPB / SportSG / Town Councils to consolidate the data, supplemented with crowdsourced corrections.
## Stakeholder Buy-in
HPB is interested in the proposed solution and features post-hackathon. The work is closely linked with their MVP feature launching in June, and we will be using parts of their prototype to test with users.
# Solutioning
## Problem-Solution Fit
Our survey shows that people thinking of starting value convenient, affordable fitness facilities. Fitness corners already meet both criteria (free to use, near home). But they perceive fitness corners as unable to meet their goals, because they don't know how to use the equipment to get there. GetStrong! Singapore proves that fitness corners can meet their goals, by showing them how.
## Functionality
 Four key flows:
- Onboarding: Discover the user's goals, so the app can suggest a fitness routine at a corner that matches.
- Discover: Users find fitness corners around them that have the equipment needed for their routine, and see the routine that matches each corner.
- Set goals and create a personalised plan: Users choose how often they want to go, how long, and on which days. The app builds a personalised plan around their availability — longer sessions on days they have more time, shorter ones when they don't.
- Learn how to use the corner: Videos and tutorials show users how to do each activity using the equipment at the corner.
## User Experience
We make users' fitness goals feel achievable, by creating a personalised plan that shows them where and how they can meet their fitness goals using the equipment at fitness corners around them.

We do this through personalisation grounded in self-efficacy theory: when users see a plan that fits their actual life, with sessions sized to the time they have, they form the belief they can succeed.
Video tutorials make it easier and more approachable to learn how to use the equipment.

# Impact & Next Steps
## Success Metrics
North Star metric: Number of first-time fitness corner sessions completed via the platform per month.
Supporting metrics:
- Install-to-first-session conversion rate.
- 7-day return rate.
- Corner usage uplift, measured against the NSPS 2025 baseline of 19%.
## Expected Impact
Short term: Hand off findings, prototype, and feature recommendations to HPB for inclusion in the next launch of the Healthy 365 app. This supports HPB's MVP work, which has not had dedicated resourcing for concept testing with users --> our research and prototype directly fill that gap.

Medium term:
Pilot with target-persona users in the North to validate the first-visit conversion funnel. Establish a fitness corner equipment data-partnership with HPB / SportSG / Town Councils to get fitness corner data coverage. 

Long-term:
Roll out the full vision: 30-day guided programme, lifestyle-integration features (time-window onboarding, weather-adaptive plans), and intrinsic-motivation features (personal records, identity-building, surprise sessions). Establish neighbourhood fitness corners as a first-choice venue for beginner strength training in Singapore, alongside parks (currently 68%) and gyms.
