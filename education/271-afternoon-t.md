## Summary

## Full Write-Up

# Problem Statement
## Users
120,000 secondary school students to begin, and all subsequent cohorts. All secondary school teachers.
## Use Case
A student-facing holistic development surface could transform how ~120,000 secondary students own their growth journey, strengthen the feedback loop between students and teachers, and make MOE's vision of "Learn More, Test Less" tangible in every student's daily experience.
## Pain Point
We solve a few problems including:

Fragmented journeys: Students experience holistic development as disconnected fragments — a VIA form here, a wellbeing check-in there, a career quiz elsewhere — with no single place to make sense of who they are becoming.
Lost student voice: Without a student-owned surface, reflections, interests, and aspirations go uncaptured. Teachers make decisions about students without the student's self-reported input at the table.
## Consequence
Missed early signals: Wellbeing concerns, motivation dips, and identity struggles surface late — because students have no low-friction way to self-check-in, and teachers have no always-on window into how students are really doing.
Aspirational without infrastructure: The 21CC refresh, EAGLES expansion, FSBB rollout, and new SEC exam all assume students can plan, track, and reflect on their holistic growth — but the digital infrastructure to make this possible simply doesn't exist.
# User Research and Market Analysis
## User Research
We conducted real-user research (FGDs, 1:1 user interviews) with students, teachers and MOE HQ members to understand the fit between the experiences that our students need, the educational direction of their educators and schools, and the educational strategies & policies of MOE HQ teams.

The key challenge we've synthesized here, is that students go through many developmental experiences, but growth is fragmented and often invisible to them. This led us to believe that what our students actually lack are not more digital experiences, but safe, low-friction ways to connect experiences into meaning over time.
## De-risking
We considered risks most pertinent to MOE HQ on all things Well-being & Holistic Development, including the problems surrounding student privacy, educator workload, burden of knowledge, lack of student adoption (e.g. on reflections fatigue), trust issues and building a sense of safety within (not outside) the school system.
The risks of leaving certain kinds of student issues or sentiments unactioned and unresolved, was considered too great to grant full-privacy to students; while also deviating from MOE objectives to strengthen teacher-student relationships. 
To circumvent these risks, we adopted a few measures to incrementally build trust, joy, engagement, rapport and meaning for our kids.
## Stakeholder Buy-in
We are working across Student Development Curriculum Division in MOE (SDCD1), with stakeholders/master specialists across Student Development Coordination Office (SDCO), Education & Career Guidance Branch (ECGB), Guidance Branch (GB), Citizenship & Character Education Branch (CCEB) etc.
# Solutioning
## Problem-Solution Fit
My World is a companion app that functions as a digital third space for students — a place to capture school moments multi-modally, and have those moments synthesised into insights about their identity, relationships, and choices. It is the missing layer that makes the policy aspiration ("Learn More, Test Less") operable at the level of the individual student.
Each design move we made maps to a specific pain point.
- Fragmented journeys - A student-owned surface that captures experiences from across CCAs, lessons, VIA, and informal life — then weaves them into one evolving portrait of self, backed by MOE frameworks (VIPS, CCE, ECG).
- Lost student voice - Answered by a 70% student-initiated capture model, where students regularly lead fun, lightweight reflections across minor but significant moments, and teachers lead structured reflections & help students sense-make their holistic development (& choices) at major moments (wellbeing weeks, ECG fairs, subject combination decisions).
- Missed early signals - Answered by background sense-making agents that surface patterns continuously (& quietly, without adding teacher workload), while evaluator agents flag risk & concerns to key school personnel for consideration.
- Aspirational without infrastructure is answered by positioning My World not as another app, but potentially as the connective tissue across the existing ecosystem of MOE strategies (SEConnect, ECG tools & frameworks, SLS etc. ) — a sense-making layer that can ingest MOE HQ strategies into helping student grow holistically (with intent) and provide the feedback/insights to HQ & schools
- Building Trust - Our research showed that students are already finding ways to make sense of their development -just in ways that are difficult for teachers and school personnel to reach. They turn to friends, different online tools, even ChatGPT. My World does not try to recreate trust where none exists; it earns it by feeling unmistakably different from past MOE surfaces and makes the process of giving insights on their lives.. rewarding.
The joy, sense of meaning and purpose we've engineered into our app will help us establish lasting, genuine and authentic student engagement that engenders the kind of student agency we'd like to see in our product.
## Functionality
What are your key user flows/features?
1. Lightweight Capture (mobile-first). Students open the app and capture a school moment in under a minute — voice, video, photo, or text. A gentle prompt helps them describe what happened and why it mattered.
  1. Quantity over quality: a basketball win, a difficult group project, a quiet moment in CCA all qualify. The bar is "would I want to remember this?", not "is this profound?".
1. Evolving Inner World (the home surface). Every captured moment becomes part of a living, customisable landscape — an island that grows with the student.
  1. Students drew the comparison to Animal Crossing in interviews; what they responded to was ownership and visibility, not gamification. The world functions as a memory map: students can revisit any moment, see how moments cluster, and feel the accumulation of their own becoming.
1. Continuous Sensemaking (the portrait of self). Behind the scenes, a multi-agent system harmonises moments into a knowledge graph of the student. An Identity Agent (mapped to VIPS, Big Five) tracks who the student is becoming; a Relationships Agent tracks empathy, peer support, and TSR signals; a Choices Agent tracks creative, adaptive, and inventive thinking; an Orchestrator Agent resolves domain overlaps and updates the portrait.
  1. Outputs are surfaced selectively for the student to validate ("does this feel true to you?") — and only then enter the persistent portrait.
  1. Around these three sit two enabling flows: Multiple Initiation Points (teacher prompts at school camps, ECG career fair, subject combination decisions; students self-initiate the rest), and
  1. Pathways Exploration (a Cartographer agent connects identity to ECG, subject combinations, and possible future selves — including a "talk to future self" surface).
  1. Underneath all of this, evaluator agents (Quality + Safety) act as guardrails, flagging dangerous content while leaving the student in control of disclosure.
## User Experience
Every design choice traced back to a single research insight: students do not lack digital experiences, they lack low-friction ways to connect experiences into meaning over time. So we removed friction wherever it existed.
Capture is multi-modal and student-driven — voice for moments that resist words, photo for visual memory, text for the introspective. Most captures complete in under a minute. The reflection prompts that follow are scaffolded, not interrogative: they help the student describe their own experience rather than answering a survey.
The visual language is deliberately unlike past MOE surfaces, similar to current digital app experiences student enjoy. We picked an immersive, game-like aesthetic because students told us existing tools felt "boring" and "school-like". 
Students described the prototype as "feels like a journal, a diary" and "not like other MOE apps." We treat that distance as a feature, not a branding decision — it is what makes the app feel safe to use honestly.
Initiation is calibrated: 80% student-led, 20% teacher-initiated. Students own the rhythm of capture; teachers prompt only at major moments where the institutional context adds value (school camps, OBS, subject combo discussions). The system rarely interrupts — it works in the background, surfacing syntheses only when there is something worth showing.
Mobile-first for capture (in-the-moment, ambient); web for sense-making (richer visualisations, longer dwell-time). Students never have to switch surfaces in the moment that matters.
Safety is built into the substrate, not the surface. Evaluator agents flag harm, but the student retains agency over disclosure level — they choose what teachers and the system see. This is critical: trust is the precondition for honest capture, and honest capture is the precondition for everything else.
## User Validation
What feedback did users give about the product? What worked well and what required improvement?
We tested with 28 students, 10 teachers, and 5 schools across the secondary and post-secondary spectrum (St Andrew's SS, NUS High, Hwa Chong, Bedok South, Junyuan, and others), through a mix of 1:1 user interviews, FGDs, school site visits, and a feedback bazaar with HQ.
From students, three sentiments recurred verbatim across the prototype walkthroughs: "Feels like a journal, a diary.""Easy and convenient." "Not like other MOE apps." Students were drawn in by the customisable island metaphor ("make it ours", "like Animal Crossing") and responded positively to multi-modal capture — particularly voice, which felt closer to how they actually think.
From teachers, the strongest signals were around enabling them to facilitate student development without significantly adding to their workload. They liked that My World could surface early signals on wellbeing and motivation without adding to their workload, and they liked that the app sparked genuine student responses, reflections and aided students in iteratively being able to express their own thoughts better, with the ability to gradually build up to meta-cognitive reflections.
From MOE HQ stakeholders at the feedback bazaar, the strongest signal was ecosystem potential: HQ teams immediately suggested extensions (career pathway exploration, integration with World of Work, MySEI) — a sign that the surface speaks the language of the ecosystem it is trying to fit into.
# Impact & Next Steps
## Success Metrics
Adoption & engagement (leading indicators). Weekly active students; captures per student per week; multi-modal usage rate (% of students who use ≥2 modalities); session length distribution.
Depth of sensemaking (the value layer). % of synthesised insights validated by students as "feels true to me"; number of moments revisited per student per term; rate of student-initiated vs teacher-initiated captures (target: 80/20).
Outcomes (lagging indicators). Self-reported identity coherence (validated scale, administered termly); teacher-reported quality of growth conversations; time-to-detection of wellbeing concerns (compared against existing channels); pathway exploration engagement at major choice points (subject combo, JC/Poly, post-sec).
## Expected Impact
Short term (0–12 months) — POC validated, foundations laid. Pilot with 2–3 schools (one secondary, ideally one with an existing pastoral care strength). Students engage 1–2x weekly; teachers find the early-signal value real without added workload; SDCD validates that the sensemaking layer aligns with CCE and ECG curriculum in practice, not just in theory. Output: a credible product–market fit signal, a refined evaluator agent, and a working integration pattern with one existing surface (likely TCI or MySEI).
Medium term (12–24 months) — POV, then early scale. Expansion to 10–20 schools as a Proof of Value. Measurable improvement in students' self-reported identity coherence at choice points (subject combo, JC/Poly decisions). Reduced reflection fatigue across surfaces by consolidating what currently lives in fragmented forms (VIA, wellbeing check-ins, career quizzes) into the single My World surface. Student-voice data begins informing 21CC refresh and EAGLES expansion at the policy level. Teachers report higher-quality growth conversations because they enter them with the student's self-reported portrait.
