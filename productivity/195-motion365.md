## Summary

MoveLah! is a workplace wellness companion that helps employees improve posture, reduce body strain, and stay active throughout the workday.

## Full Write-Up

# Problem Statement
## Users
MoveLah! is designed for desk-bound office workers, especially public officers and GovTech employees who spend long hours working at their desks.
## Use Case
Office workers are trying to stay focused and productive during the workday, but often remain seated for long periods without noticing their posture or remembering to take movement breaks.
This problem happens mainly during desk work, especially when users are working on laptops or external monitors for extended periods.
## Pain Point
The key pain point is that desk-bound workers are experiencing physical discomfort from prolonged sitting, but they do not consistently notice or act on it during the workday.
## Consequence
If this problem is not solved, workers may continue experiencing stiffness, poor posture, neck and shoulder pain, and lower back discomfort. Over time, this can affect productivity, daily wellbeing, and potentially lead to higher long-term health maintenance costs.
# User Research and Market Analysis
## User Research
We conducted a workplace wellness survey with 9 respondents to validate the problem and understand desk-working habits.
### Survey Results
- 77.8% rated their neck and shoulder discomfort at 3 or above.
- 77.8% rated their lower back discomfort at 3 or above.
- 77.8% felt moderately to extremely stiff or physically drained at the end of a workday.
- 55.6% sit for 6 hours or more per workday.
- 77.8% only take movement breaks every 2–3 hours, or only during lunch/end of day.
- 55.6% rated their posture awareness as only 2 out of 5.
- 62.5% of valid responses said discomfort interrupts their work at 3 or above.
### Key Insight
The survey shows that desk workers commonly experience discomfort but often lack timely posture awareness and consistent movement habits during work. This supports the need for MoveLah!, a lightweight web app that provides posture nudges and guided micro-exercises.
## De-risking
Privacy risk: Users may be uncomfortable with webcam-based posture detection.
Mitigation: Posture detection runs locally in the browser. No video is stored or uploaded.
Accuracy risk: Posture detection may not be medically accurate.
Mitigation: MoveLah! is positioned as a wellness companion, not a medical diagnosis tool.
Engagement risk: Users may stop using the app after a few days.
Mitigation: The app uses streaks, short daily programmes, and leaderboard motivation.
Interruption risk: Notifications may disrupt work.
Mitigation: Nudges are subtle, positive, and only triggered after sustained poor posture.

# Solutioning
## Problem-Solution Fit
MoveLah! addresses the core problem by helping desk workers become more aware of their posture and encouraging them to move regularly during the workday.
- Poor posture goes unnoticed → AI posture monitoring detects posture issues and provides subtle reminders.
- Users forget to take breaks → MoveLah! prompts short movement breaks at the right time.
- Exercises feel boring → Mini games make movement more fun and engaging.
- Users lack motivation → Streaks and leaderboards encourage consistency.
- Webcam privacy concerns → Posture detection runs locally in the browser, with no video stored or uploaded.
### Functionality
MoveLah! is a desktop-first web app with the following key features:
- Dashboard
Shows posture status, daily streak, programme progress, and leaderboard.
- AI Posture Monitoring
Uses webcam-based posture detection to identify poor posture and trigger gentle nudges.
- Mobility Programmes
Users can join guided programmes such as Back Pain Reset, Neck & Shoulder Relief, and Desk Mobility Starter.
- Mini Game Modes
Users can complete movement-based mini games:
  - PunchQuest — shadow boxing / air punch movement.
  - Shoulder Smash! — neck and shoulder mobility.
  - Reach & Rally — stretching, grabbing, and reaching.
- Streaks & Leaderboard
Tracks completed sessions and encouraged friendly competition across individuals, departments, and GovTech-wide groups.
### User Experience
MoveLah! is designed to be simple, non-intrusive, and easy to use during work.
- Users can start posture monitoring in one click.
- Nudges are subtle and positive, not disruptive.
- Exercises and mini games are short, so they fit into quick work breaks.
- The app uses a clear dashboard so users can easily see progress and next actions.
- No login is required for the POC, allowing users to experience the product immediately.
- Privacy messaging is clearly shown so users know their webcam feed stays on-device.

# Impact & Next Steps
## Success Metrics
Northstar Metrics - Percentage of active users who complete at least one healthy movement action per workday.
This directly measures whether MoveLah! is achieving its main goal: helping officers build a daily habit of moving more and taking care of their posture during work.
Supporting Metrics - Daily Active Movement Minutes per Officer
This measures actual behaviour change, not just app visits. It shows whether users are spending meaningful time on posture checks, exercises, or mini games.
Supporting Metrics - Average Sessions per User
This tracks how often users return to MoveLah!. A higher number of sessions shows that the app is useful, engaging, and becoming part of the user’s workday routine.
## Expected Impact
We aim to creates a healthier, happier, and more productive GovTech workforce, with a scalable model for the wider public sector
Key Outcomes:
- Short Term: Deliver ≥15–25% increase in movement levels with ≥80% engagement within a 20-user pilot cohort
- Medium Term: Sustain ≥20% improvement in activity levels while expanding participation across teams
- Long Term: Scale a measurable, data-driven wellness model across GovTech and the broader public sector
