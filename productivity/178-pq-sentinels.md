## Summary

An AI-assisted platform to extract, track, coordinate, and manage Parliamentary Questions (PQs) with full visibility from inbox to submission.

## Full Write-Up

# Problem Statement
## Users
PQ Team (2 officers), Divisions and Senior Management
## Use Case
The COD Registry Team (aka PQ Coordinator) needs to capture and route all MTI
Parliamentary Questions (PQ) to correct divisions, ensure Desk Officers upload PQ responses to Hansard on time, and provide timely status updates to management
when managing PQ sittings from start to finish,
but currently relies on fragmented manual processes, requiring over 260 manhours per year for PQ management, including extracting PQs from parliamentary clerk emails, coordinating responses across divisions, sending reminders to Desk Officers, and generating daily status reports.
This results in significant operational inefficiencies and prevents MTI from achieving consistent and on–time PQ responses with minimal human intervention.
## Pain Point
The manual coordination work and tracking to ensure timely clearance and submission of PQ responses.
## Consequence
Estimated average time taken for PQ team to handle approximately 130 PQs per year (end-to-end process): over 260 manhours.
# User Research and Market Analysis
## User Research
We ran a co-creation workshop with a small focus group of PQ Coordinators, walking
through the Double Diamond design process to map the "As-Is" PQ
journey. From there, we collectively reframed key pain points into "How
Might We" (HMW) statements, which served as springboards for ideation.
Finally, we used dot voting to rank ideas — giving us a quantitative signal
within a qualitative setting that reveals what users value most.
Two key insights from PQ Coordinators:
A. The "Chain Reaction" Risk
There is extreme anxiety around the manual extraction stage. "If
this is wrong, the rest will be wrong." The system needs high accuracy
automated extraction of MTI related PQs and ensures PQs reach the correct
divisions immediately.
B. The "Status Chasing" Burden
Coordinators are currently acting as "human alarm clocks," manually reminding Desk Officers via emails and at their desks. A shift in ownership is needed. Desk Officers should manage the process, while the system handles the "nagging" via a central dashboard and automated reminders.

# Solutioning
## Problem-Solution Fit
How does your product specifically address the pain points identified?
Addresses fragmented and manual PQ workflows by leveraging AI to automatically extract PQs directed at MTI, streamlining coordination and reminders, and providing real-time visibility for management through a unified dashboard.
## Functionality
What are your key user flows/features?
1. PQ notification emails from the Parliament Clerk are received, containing questions for multiple ministries.
1. Questions directed at MTI are automatically extracted, and corresponding cases are created in the system.
1. MTI PQ coordinator reviews and confirms assignments with divisions, with updates reflected in the system.
1. Assignment notifications and automated reminders are sent to assignees (desk officers) to ensure timely follow-up.
1. Assignees can update the clearance status directly, keeping progress up to date.
1. A unified dashboard provides real-time visibility of all PQs for upcoming sitting, highlighting overall status and areas requiring intervention.
## User Experience
How did you make the experience seamless/intuitive for the end user?
1. For PQ coordinators: Minimises manual effort by providing a single platform to manage all PQs and track their status.
1. For desk officers: Simplifies status updates with just two clicks. Email reminders include all the necessary instructions.
1. For Management (and PAs): Provides real-time visibility of all PQs status and ease of retrieval for relevant PQ information.

# Impact & Next Steps
## Success Metrics
What is your "North Star" metric?
% of PQs managed end-to-end on PQ Tracker – A high number indicates that our
platform is useful for PQ coordinator, desk officers and management.
Average manhours per PQ – reduction in manhours spent means the platform has successfully reduce the manual coordination work for staff.
## Expected Impact
What outcomes do you expect this to be able to deliver in the short/medium/long term if your product is launched to users?
We target to reduce time spent for coordinating PQs in MTI by 50%, or 130 hours / year.
In the long run, we would like to automate more sub-processes with AI to further reduce time spent by desk officers and approvers in crafting and clearing PQ responses.
