## Summary

500 GovTechies lose 15 minutes a week each searching for open seats in PDD.

## Full Write-Up

# Problem Statement
## Users
500 GovTechies going to PDD.
## Use Case
Seat availability affects the speed in which a user can settle down to start their day - it can also affect planning and decisions on whether to go to PDD.
## Pain Point
Given updated schedules, there can be enough seats - but the distribution and availability is fluid.
## Consequence
More clarity on seat availability will help in future planning and observing team building usage patterns -- this can better forecast GovTech's building needs.
# User Research and Market Analysis
## User Research
We've observed fellow colleagues scanning and pacing the PDD floors for a suitable workspace.
From our own experience, the uncertainty on the availability of seats can deter teams from meeting in office or individuals from coming in.
## De-risking
### Ease of Access
Office goers may find it cumbersome to log into, or even access another website to get information on something as mundane as seat availability. We have a non-login interface that can be casted onto other form factors such as office monitors and other screens in office lobbies.
### User Privacy
One extension of this project is to support team clustering - i.e. direct officer goers to where their team mates are seated. We wanted to maintain the privacy of our users so we avoided using methods that would involve video capture or uploading of personal information. For team seat clustering purposes, we cannot avoid collecting team composition information in the system and identifying office goers at some part of the user journey.
## Stakeholder Buy-in
GovTech CIO Office has indicated interest in supporting the system post-hackathon.
# Solutioning
## Problem-Solution Fit
The website will show seat availability live. This allows GovTechies to find their preferred available seat once they have arrived in office, or to plan trips based on current activity and historical data.
## Functionality
### Basic
I. User accesses live view with map
II. User navigates to desired seat
### Advanced
I. User logs into website (or scans in on another form factor)
II. Application highlights nearest spaces to their teammates
## User Experience
We wanted as little inputs from the user as possible so our base user flow does not require authentication. We also avoided solutions that required physical inputs from the users to indicate seat occupancy.
## User Validation
We had received feedback with regards to grouping teams together, seat suggestion and seat booking. These use cases continue to shape our product and are key expansion pathways.
# Impact & Next Steps
## Success Metrics
### For Office Goers
Time spent finding a seat after arrival - Reduce time to find most eligible available seat.
### (Advanced) For Teams
Time spent understanding seat availability on any given day - Time spent researching or coordinating with other teams should go down.
Accuracy of seat availability predictions - When teams do decide to come in, how often are they faced with seat availability issues - This number should go down.
## Expected Impact
What outcomes do you expect this to be able to deliver in the short/medium/long term if your product is launched to users?
### Short-Term
Office goers will experience a decrease in the time taken to get seated from the time of arrival by an average of 5 minutes. Assuming 200 office goers a day, that amounts to 200 x 5 minutes = 1000 minutes saved per day, or ~17 hours saved per day.
They will also be more satisfied with their seat as they know it is the best possible one available.
### Medium-Term
Organisers of team office days will experience an increase in productivity - from gains in time saved coordinating (blindly) with other teams.
Teams will experience an increase in productivity from the reduction of disruptions to team members who cannot find workspaces in office.
### Long-Term
Organisation will see an increase in intra and inter-team collaboration and office goer quality of life, and from that, ideation and productivity.
